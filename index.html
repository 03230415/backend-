Introduction

I am Yangchay, a third-year student pursuing Bachelor of Business Intelligence at Gedu College of Business Studies. As part of my internship program, I worked on a project titled “Telecommunication Service Usage in Bhutan.” This project focused on analyzing user behavior and internet usage patterns across different needs and locations within the country. The aim of this web platform is to serve as a compass that helps users identify the most suitable network service based on their requirements.


Situation Overview

When I got enrolled as an intern under Bhutan Data Scientist Pvt. Ltd. doing remote work, I depended heavily on mobile data for everything—online research, Zoom meetings, and everyday online activities.

Because I was using mobile data for almost all my activities, the expenses were high. I was spending around Nu. 699 in a week, and even when I tried a Nu. 1199 plan, I still had to recharge twice a month, and it still barely lasted. In total, I ended up spending more than Nu. 2500.

Problem Faced

I wanted to save some money and switch to Wi-Fi service or get a plan with higher value that could save me a few bucks, but I often struggled with uncertainty. I was confused about which telecommunication service to choose or whether I should continue with my current plan. My thoughts kept going back and forth, especially when trying to manage internet costs. I even worried, “What if my investment in Wi-Fi fails?”

When I inquired at a service counter, I was advised to reconsider carefully. I was told that if the internet strength in my area is weak, the Wi-Fi performance would also be poor, and in such cases, refunds would not be possible. At the same time, I heard rumors circulating that Wi-Fi services were no longer as reliable as before, with some users requesting refunds and others complaining about cost and stability on social media. This made me even more uncertain and overwhelmed, and for nearly a month, I kept going back and forth with my decision.

Over time, I had to become more careful about how I used the internet. I started limiting my entertainment activities like gaming, online videos, and watching dramas because of data constraints.

I was always stressed about running out of data and unhappy about not being able to watch videos or play music freely which also affected my work efficiency.

Solution

Later, my cousin suggested that I try using Wi-Fi. She kindly let me use her router, and I started working through it.

The difference was clear.

With Wi-Fi, I could do my work smoothly, attend Zoom meetings without interruption, and freely use the internet for research and entertainment such as YouTube, TikTok, games, and dramas without worrying about running out of data.

It also became more affordable, costing Nu. 1450 per month, which was much better than what I was spending on mobile data.

This experience taught me that the type of internet connection matters a lot depending on how it is used. For remote work and daily usage, Wi-Fi was simply more stable, practical, and cost-effective for me.

Project Background

🎓
Intern Researcher
Data Practitioner -
 Turning Data into Insights

As part of my college program, I completed a six-month internship at Bhutan Data Scientists Pvt. Ltd. This project explores telecommunications service usage in Bhutan, with a focus on customer preferences and satisfaction. The website aims to provide personalized service recommendations based on user's needs.

The project focuses on reducing Bhutan's digital divide by understanding connectivity gaps and promoting fair access, to telecommunications services across all 20 dzongkhags.

Project Highlights

  • Understanding Mobile data package & Wi-Fi Usage PatternsExamining the internet service usage pattern across urban and rural area of Bhutan, including what is their primary purpose and their needs based on occupation.

Key Findings

📱

Mobile Dominance

Bhutanese access the internet primarily via mobile devices, with 4G/LTE being the most used standard and introduction of 5G service in urban central.

🌄

Rural Gap

A significant issue regarding network connectivity and realibility shows a potential for Starlink's satellite service to help bridge in remote dzongkhags and curb the gap.

⭐

Satisfaction Trends

Average satisfaction scores hover around 3/5, with network reliability and affordable pricing cited as the top improvement priorities.

🚀

Growth Opportunity

Bhutan is strengthening digital infrastructure through the Digital Drukyul initiative, expanding fibre networks and improving rural connectivity.
